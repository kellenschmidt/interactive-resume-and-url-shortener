Summary

The First Year Engineering Design Robot Competition was a semester long class in which we were grouped with engineers from multiple disciplines and tasked with building a robot to autonomously navigate an obstacle course while completing objectives such as locating a soil source, testing percent water concentration, and measuring wind speed and ambient temperature.

What I did

Worked in a Scrum group of 6 engineers to construct and program the robot from scratch

  • Helped contruct the the robot using MicroRax (metal robotics kit) and ultrasonic, bump, light, and GPS sensors
  • Used object-oriented Java to program the robot to autonomously navigate the course and complete the objectives
  • Finished 2nd out of 16 teams in the final competition

Features

  • Class was centered around Scrum methodology, including sprints, standups, and retrospectives
  • I frequently acted as Scrum master to organizing the team and help alleviate blockers
  • Robot could adapt to changing course structure and avoid other robots on the course
  • Sensors were custom calibrated to within 5% accuracy
